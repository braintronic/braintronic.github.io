Importing Machinery Spare Parts

Your Gateway to Genuine Industrial Parts

Importing advanced industrial equipment and spare parts—such as tea color sorters, compressors, air dryers, and Variable Frequency Drives (VFDs)—is a critical part of supporting Sri Lanka’s fast-paced and export-focused tea industry. As local manufacturing for these specialized items is limited, reliable imports ensure continued efficiency, quality, and factory uptime.

What Can Be Imported?

  • Complete Machinery Units
    • Tea Color Sorters (e.g. Anysort, Thaiho, Mayer, Satake)
    • Rotary Screw & Piston Compressors
    • Desiccant and Refrigerated Air Dryers
    • VFD Panels & Integrated Motor Controllers
  • Spare Parts & Consumables
    • Sensor modules, PLC boards, ejector valves for color sorters
    • Compressor parts: filters, belts, pistons, crankshafts, control panels
    • Desiccant media, filters, dryers’ solenoid valves
    • VFD fuses, control boards, IGBT modules, cables

Importing Process (Step-by-Step)

  1. Sourcing & Supplier Agreements
    • Select reliable OEM manufacturers or authorized distributors
    • Confirm technical compatibility with local systems (voltage, controls, climate ratings)
    • Negotiate warranty, lead time, and post-sale support
  2. Documentation Preparation
    • Proforma Invoice & Purchase Order
    • HS Codes (Harmonized System codes) for customs classification
    • Manufacturer’s specs and datasheets
    • Certificate of Origin
    • CE/FCC/RoHS compliance if applicable
  3. Freight & Shipping
    • Choose between air freight (for urgent parts) and sea freight (for full machines)
    • Ensure items are packed for high humidity and salt-air environments
    • Shipping insurance is highly recommended
  4. Customs Clearance in Sri Lanka
    • Register as an importer with the Sri Lanka Customs Department
    • Submit through ASYCUDA (the digital customs clearance system)
    • Pay applicable import duties, VAT, PAL (Port and Airport Levy), and CESS
    • HS Code Examples:
      • 843710: Color sorting machines
      • 841480: Compressors
      • 841939: Industrial dryers
      • 850440: VFDs and static converters
  5. Delivery & Installation
    • Partner with logistics companies for inland transport to factory sites
    • Engage technical teams (like Braintronic) for safe installation, calibration, and commissioning

Key Considerations

  • Taxes & Duties
    • Import duties range from 5%–30% depending on item classification
    • VAT (15%) applies to most imported machinery
    • Exemptions may apply for BOI-approved companies or items used for export production
  • Standards & Compatibility
    • Machines must support 220V/50Hz or 400V/3Ph electrical systems
    • Equipment should be tolerant to dust, vibration, and tropical humidity
  • Warranties & Serviceability
    • Ensure imported items come with international warranties
    • Spare parts should be readily available or pre-stocked locally

Common Challenges

  • Delays in customs due to incorrect HS codes or incomplete documentation
  • Hidden costs in CIF pricing (freight, insurance, inland delivery)
  • Compatibility issues with older local systems if not pre-assessed

Countries Commonly Imported From

  • China (Anysort, Thaiho, Shanghai brand compressors, VFDs, dryers)
  • India (ELGi compressors, domestic PLC/VFD brands)
  • Germany (Kaeser, Siemens VFDs, high-end dryers)
  • Japan (Satake color sorters, advanced control systems)
  • Singapore / Malaysia (Distributor hubs for Asian market)
